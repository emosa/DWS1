Puerto Rican Style Candied Coconut Rice

Sweet rice pudding serve for desert throughout the holiday season in Puerto Rico.

12 Servings.

Ingredients

  • 1½ rice cups
  • 4¼ coconut milk cups
  • ¾ coconut milk cups (reserve to use at the end)
  • 1½ sugar cups
  • ½ raisins cup
  • 1½ salt teaspoons
  • 3 ounces of ginger
  • 4 cinnamon sticks
  • 5 whole gloves
  • ½ teaspoon of nutmeg

Instructions

  1. Soak rice in water for about two hours. Add enough water to cover all rice.
  2. Twenty minutes before the rice fishing soaking combine 4¼ coconut milk cups, ginger, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg and cloves in a medium size casserole.
  3. Boil ingredients in casserole over high heat. Reduce heat to medium, cover and cook for fifteen minutes.
  4. Drain rice thoroughly and add to casserole. Stir and bring to boil over medium heat. Reduce heat to low and cook until rice is completely dried, without stirring.
  5. Add raising and sugar, stir and bring to boil over medium heat. Reduce heat to low and cook for fifteen minutes, without stirring.
  6. Add the reserve ¾ coconut milk cups and stir. Lower hear to medium and boil for about half hour, or until the rice dries out again. Turn rice occasionally. Make sure to scrape the bottom of the casserole.
  7. Turn off the heat, remove cloves and cinnamon sticks. Spoon rice into a flat platter. Allow to cool at room temperature. Refrigerate until is ready to serve. This is served cold.
